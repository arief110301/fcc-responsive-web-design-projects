John Muir

The Father of National Parks

Profile

John Muir, also known as the ‘Father of National Parks’, was a famous American conservationist, writer, naturalist and explorer, who promoted the conservation of wildlife in the United States of America and around the world. He effectively lobbied Congress to pass the National Park Bill. His activism helped the preservation of Sequoia National Park and the Yosemite Valley. He co-founded the Sierra Club, which today has over 1.3 million members and has helped establish a number of wildlife reserves.

Chronology

  • Born - April 21, 1838
  • Immigrated to the United States - 1849
  • Enrolled in University of Wisconsin-Madison - 1860
  • Left Wisconsin to explore Canadian wilderness - 1864
  • Undertook 1,000 mile walk from Kentucky to Florida - 1867
  • Traveled to Cuba - 1868
  • Moved to San Francisco, California - 1868
  • Moved to Yosemite, California - 1868
  • Offered teaching position at Harvard, declines - 1871
  • Discovers active glacier below Merced peak - 1871
  • Experiences Lone Pine earthquake - 1872
  • Travels to Alaska - 1879
  • Gets married - 1880
  • Co-founds the Sierra Club - 1892
  • Accompanies President Theodore Roosevelt to Yosemite - 1903
  • Dies of pneumonia - December 24, 1914
